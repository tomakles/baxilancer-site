Everything You Need to Know About GPS Time Tracking

GPS time tracking is a modern technology that uses Satellite-based Global Positioning System (GPS) data to monitor and record work hours based on physical location. For businesses and individuals whose work happens in the field—like construction sites, client offices, or multiple service locations—it offers a level of accuracy and automation that manual timers simply cannot match. A GPS time tracking app works by creating "geofences" around specific work locations. When a user enters this virtual boundary, the app detects their presence and can automatically start a work session. When they leave the boundary, the session stops.

How Automatic Time Tracking Works

The magic of automatic time tracking lies in its "set it and forget it" approach. Traditional time tracking requires the user to remember to press a button at the start and end of every task. In many real-world scenarios, however, this manual step is frequently forgotten. Automatic time tracking removes the human error element. Using geofencing technology, the app monitors the device's location in the background. When the GPS coordinates match a predefined work project's address, the system triggers the time recording.

One of the most innovative features of Baxilancer is the 10-minute smart delay. This delay ensures that the app doesn't start tracking time for brief, accidental visits—such as picking up a tool you forgot, a quick 5-minute site check, or just driving past the location. Only if the user remains within the geofence for longer than 10 minutes does the app retroactively start the clock from the moment of arrival. This ensures that only actual, sustained work time is recorded, providing a more professional and accurate log for both the worker and the client.
